Service Disclaimer

Important facts about the nature and limits of a WorldIDP translation.

Last updated: July 6, 2026
Key pointsPrivate serviceOriginal requiredNo acceptance promiseNo legal advice
WorldIDP provides translation only. We do not issue government documents, licences, official permits or driving privileges.

1. Independent private service

WORLDIDP INTERNATIONAL LLC is an independent private company. It is not affiliated with, endorsed by or acting on behalf of a government, embassy, consulate, motor vehicle department, police authority, licensing body, the United Nations or a rental company.

2. Translation does not replace the original

A WorldIDP translation is a language aid based on information visible in a source document. It does not prove the source document is authentic or current, does not change its validity and must not be used instead of the original. A driving-licence translation does not grant authority to drive and does not add vehicle categories.

3. Acceptance is decided by others

Laws and recipient requirements vary by country, state, institution and intended use. We do not guarantee that any government authority, court, insurer, rental business, employer or other recipient will accept a private translation. Before ordering, ask the intended recipient whether it requires a certified, sworn, notarized, consular or government-issued translation or permit.

4. Source limitations

The translation depends on source accuracy and readability. We may not detect forgery, expiry, alteration or omitted context. Names and transliteration can have multiple valid spellings. Customers must review delivered work and report apparent discrepancies promptly.

5. No legal or immigration advice

Website content and customer support are general information, not legal, immigration, licensing or regulatory advice. For official requirements, contact the relevant authority or a qualified professional.

6. Brand and third-party references

Any third-party name used to describe a possible recipient remains the property of its owner and does not imply endorsement or partnership.

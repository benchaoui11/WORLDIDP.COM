Driving in Costa Rica: What to Confirm Before You Travel

We have not completed source-backed research for Costa Rica, so this page does not state whether an International Driving Permit is required. Costa Rica drives on the right. Costa Rica is party to the 1949 Geneva Convention. Confirm current requirements with the relevant national authority and your rental provider before you drive.

Which convention applies

Costa Rica is party to the 1949 Geneva Convention. The convention determines which IDP format has a legal basis here, which is why two IDPs that look similar are not always interchangeable.

What to carry

  • Original valid national driving licence
  • Passport showing the entry stamp — this proves your authorised stay
  • Mandatory local liability insurance documentation
  • Rental agreement

Your original national driving licence is the document that grants driving privileges. A translation-support document is presented alongside it and never replaces it.

Rental desks and roadside checks in Costa Rica

At the rental desk. Mandatory local liability insurance is a legal requirement separate from any collision waiver, and it cannot be declined in favour of a credit-card policy. Many destinations reachable on a map require a high-clearance or four-wheel-drive vehicle in practice, and standard policies commonly exclude river crossings and unpaved routes.

In a roadside check. Traffic police conduct roadside checks and will ask for the passport alongside the licence, because the entry stamp is what evidences your authorised stay. Road conditions vary sharply between the Interamericana and rural routes, and unlit roads with pedestrians are common after dark.

Costa Rica driving document questions

Do you need an International Driving Permit in Costa Rica?

A valid foreign licence generally covers you for the length of your authorised tourist stay. Carry your passport with the entry stamp, because that is what evidences the period you are permitted to drive.

Do I need special insurance to drive in Costa Rica?

Yes. Mandatory local liability insurance is a legal requirement separate from any collision damage waiver, and it cannot be declined in favour of a credit-card policy.

Do I need a 4x4 in Costa Rica?

For many rural and coastal destinations, in practice yes. Standard rental policies commonly exclude river crossings and unpaved roads, so check what your agreement actually covers before choosing a route.

Do You Need an International Driving Permit in New Zealand?

Visitors may drive for up to 12 months on a valid overseas licence, but it must be in English or accompanied by an accurate English translation — an International Driving Permit is one accepted form of that translation. New Zealand is party to the 1949 Geneva Convention. Traffic drives on the left. Motorway limit 100 km/h. Sources reviewed 2026-07-23.

Who needs an IDP in New Zealand

Who this applies to. Visitors may drive for up to 12 months on a valid overseas licence, but it must be in English or accompanied by an accurate English translation — an International Driving Permit is one accepted form of that translation

Who is treated differently. Licences already issued in English need no translation, though the 12-month limit still applies

New Zealand's rule is about language, not nationality. NZTA requires an overseas licence to be in English or accompanied by an accurate English translation, and an IDP is one accepted form of that translation. The 12-month limit applies regardless of which language your licence is in.

Which convention applies

New Zealand is party to the 1949 Geneva Convention. The convention determines which IDP format has a legal basis here, which is why two IDPs that look similar are not always interchangeable.

Is a digital IDP accepted in New Zealand?

Not confirmed. No published New Zealand policy accepting digital-only driving documents was found. Carry the printed licence and translation.

This is the least documented question in this category. Most national authorities have not published a position on digital-only driving documents, and rental companies set their own rules. Carry a printed document and treat any digital copy as a convenience, not a substitute. See our explanation of what an IDP is for more.

What to carry

  • Original valid overseas driving licence
  • Accurate English translation or IDP if the licence is not in English
  • Passport
  • Rental agreement and insurance documents

Your original national driving licence is the document that grants driving privileges. A translation-support document is presented alongside it and never replaces it.

Rental desks and roadside checks in New Zealand

At the rental desk. Rental companies enforce the English-language rule at the desk and will decline a vehicle without an acceptable translation. Insurance excesses on campervans and 4WDs are high, and many policies exclude unsealed roads — which rules out a number of routes people plan to drive.

In a roadside check. New Zealand drives on the left and has relatively little motorway outside Auckland and Wellington. One-lane bridges are common on the South Island and carry priority signage that visitors routinely misread. Drivers under 20 face a zero alcohol limit.

Specific to New Zealand

  • New Zealand is party to the 1949 Geneva Convention.
  • An overseas licence must be in English or carry an accurate English translation — this is a stated NZTA requirement, not a rental preference.
  • Visitors may drive for a maximum of 12 months on an overseas licence.
  • Traffic drives on the left, and one-lane bridges with priority signage are common outside the main centres.
  • Drivers under 20 face a zero alcohol limit; drivers 20 and over are limited to 250 micrograms per litre of breath.

New Zealand driving document questions

Do you need an International Driving Permit in New Zealand?

You need your overseas licence to be in English or accompanied by an accurate English translation. An IDP is one accepted form of that translation. If your licence is already in English, you do not need one.

How long can I drive in New Zealand on an overseas licence?

Up to 12 months. After that you need a New Zealand licence, regardless of what translation you carry.

Which side of the road does New Zealand drive on?

The left. Open-road limits are generally 100 km/h and urban limits 50 km/h, with one-lane bridges common on the South Island.

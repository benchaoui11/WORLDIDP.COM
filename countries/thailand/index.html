Do You Need an International Driving Permit in Thailand?

Foreign tourists can use an IDP under the 1949 Geneva Convention, or an ASEAN licence where that applies. Thailand is party to the 1949 Geneva Convention. Traffic drives on the left. Sources reviewed 2026-07-23.

Who needs an IDP in Thailand

Who this applies to. Foreign tourists can use an IDP under the 1949 Geneva Convention, or an ASEAN licence where that applies.

Who is treated differently. Some ASEAN licence holders may be accepted under regional arrangements.

Thailand accepts an IDP issued under the 1949 Geneva Convention for foreign tourists. Some ASEAN licence holders are covered by regional arrangements instead. Anyone outside those two routes should check before driving.

Which convention applies

Thailand is party to the 1949 Geneva Convention. The convention determines which IDP format has a legal basis here, which is why two IDPs that look similar are not always interchangeable.

Is a digital IDP accepted in Thailand?

Not confirmed. No confirmed official national policy was found. Carry the printed document and original licence when a physical document is requested.

This is the least documented question in this category. Most national authorities have not published a position on digital-only driving documents, and rental companies set their own rules. Carry a printed document and treat any digital copy as a convenience, not a substitute. See our explanation of what an IDP is for more.

What to carry

  • Original valid driving licence
  • Passport or travel ID where requested
  • IDP, official translation, or private translation-support document where applicable

Your original national driving licence is the document that grants driving privileges. A translation-support document is presented alongside it and never replaces it.

Rental desks and roadside checks in Thailand

At the rental desk. Rental and scooter-hire providers in tourist areas vary widely in what they check. A provider not asking for a document does not mean the police will not.

In a roadside check. Roadside checks of foreign drivers, particularly scooter riders in tourist areas, are common. Insurance cover can also be affected if you are riding without the document the policy requires.

Specific to Thailand

  • Thailand recognises the 1949 Geneva Convention IDP.
  • ASEAN licence holders may be covered by regional arrangements.
  • Thailand drives on the left.
  • Scooter and motorcycle hire is subject to the same document rules as cars, and the licence category must cover the vehicle.

Thailand driving document questions

Do I need an International Driving Permit in Thailand?

Yes for most foreign tourists. Thai guidance points to an IDP issued under the 1949 Geneva Convention, or an ASEAN licence where regional arrangements apply.

Do I need an IDP to rent a scooter in Thailand?

The same document rules apply, and your licence category must cover motorcycles. Riding without the correct category can affect both enforcement and insurance.

Which side of the road does Thailand drive on?

Thailand drives on the left.

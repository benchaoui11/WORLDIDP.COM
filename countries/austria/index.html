Do You Need an International Driving Permit in Austria?

Licences issued outside the EU or EEA must be accompanied by an International Driving Permit or a certified German translation. Austria is party to the 1968 Vienna Convention. Traffic drives on the right. Motorway limit 130 km/h. Sources reviewed 2026-07-23.

Who needs an IDP in Austria

Who this applies to. Licences issued outside the EU or EEA must be accompanied by an International Driving Permit or a certified German translation

Who is treated differently. EU and EEA licence holders drive under EU mutual recognition rules

Austria requires more than most of its neighbours from non-EU licence holders: guidance points to an IDP or a certified German translation, not simply any translation. If you are driving an Alpine route between Germany, Austria and Italy, note that all three countries apply different rules to the same licence.

Which convention applies

Austria is party to the 1968 Vienna Convention. The convention determines which IDP format has a legal basis here, which is why two IDPs that look similar are not always interchangeable.

Is a digital IDP accepted in Austria?

Not confirmed. No published Austrian national policy on digital-only driving documents was found. Carry a printed copy.

This is the least documented question in this category. Most national authorities have not published a position on digital-only driving documents, and rental companies set their own rules. Carry a printed document and treat any digital copy as a convenience, not a substitute. See our explanation of what an IDP is for more.

What to carry

  • Original valid national driving licence
  • IDP or certified German translation for non-EU/EEA licences
  • Passport or national ID
  • Motorway vignette displayed on the windscreen
  • Winter tyres between 1 November and 15 April in wintry conditions

Your original national driving licence is the document that grants driving privileges. A translation-support document is presented alongside it and never replaces it.

Rental desks and roadside checks in Austria

At the rental desk. Winter equipment is the thing that catches visitors. Between 1 November and 15 April, vehicles must have winter tyres in wintry conditions — this is a legal requirement, not a rental upsell, and driving without them in snow can invalidate insurance and attract a fine.

In a roadside check. Austrian motorways require a vignette displayed on the windscreen, and some Alpine tunnels and passes carry separate tolls on top. The alcohol limit is 0.01 per cent for drivers in their first three years, professional drivers and moped riders, against 0.05 for everyone else.

Specific to Austria

  • Austria is party to the 1968 Vienna Convention.
  • A motorway vignette must be displayed on the windscreen; some Alpine tunnels and passes are tolled separately on top of it.
  • Winter tyres are legally required in wintry conditions between 1 November and 15 April.
  • New drivers face a 0.01 per cent alcohol limit for their first three years.
  • Non-EU licence holders need an IDP or a certified German translation, which is stricter than several neighbouring states.

Austria driving document questions

Do you need an International Driving Permit in Austria?

If your licence was issued outside the EU or EEA, yes — Austrian guidance points to an IDP or a certified German translation carried with the original licence. EU and EEA licence holders drive under mutual recognition.

Do I need a vignette to drive in Austria?

Yes, for motorways and expressways. It must be displayed on the windscreen. Some Alpine tunnels and passes carry separate tolls that the vignette does not cover.

Are winter tyres compulsory in Austria?

In wintry conditions between 1 November and 15 April, yes. This is a legal requirement rather than a rental option, and driving without them in snow can attract a fine and affect insurance cover.

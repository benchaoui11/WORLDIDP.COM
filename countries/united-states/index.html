Who needs an IDP in the United States

Who this applies to. Driving privileges for visitors are set by each state, not federally. Most states allow a visitor to drive on a valid foreign licence for a limited period, and a licence not written in English is where a translation or International Driving Permit becomes practically necessary

Who is treated differently. A licence issued in English is generally readable without a translation, though the state's time limit still applies and rental company policy can be stricter than state law

This question runs in two directions and they have different answers. **Driving into the United States on a foreign licence:** privileges are set by each state, most allow a visiting driver a limited period on a valid foreign licence, and the practical trigger for a translation is language — a licence not in English is one an officer or rental agent cannot read. **US licence holders driving abroad:** only two organisations are authorised by the U.S. Department of State to issue US International Driving Permits — the American Automobile Association and the American Automobile Touring Alliance. No third organisation can become authorised by describing itself as official. One rule catches people out in both directions: an IDP must be issued in the country that issued your licence. A UK licence holder cannot obtain a US IDP from AAA, and a US licence holder cannot obtain one abroad.

Which convention applies

The United States is party to the 1949 Geneva Convention. The convention determines which IDP format has a legal basis here, which is why two IDPs that look similar are not always interchangeable.

Is a digital IDP accepted in the United States?

Not confirmed. No US state has published a policy accepting a digital-only driving document, and the American Automobile Association states that digital IDPs are not available. Treat printed as the only reliable format.

This is the least documented question in this category. Most national authorities have not published a position on digital-only driving documents, and rental companies set their own rules. Rental desks and roadside checks in the United States

At the rental desk. Rental policy is the binding constraint for most visitors, and it is set by the company rather than the state. Several major providers require a translation for any licence not written in English regardless of what state law says. Minimum age is commonly 21, with substantial young-driver surcharges applied below 25 and some vehicle classes excluded entirely. A passport is routinely requested alongside the licence from foreign visitors.

In a roadside check. Enforcement is by state and local agencies, and rules differ meaningfully across state lines. The alcohol limit is 0.08 per cent in 49 states; Utah alone applies 0.05 per cent. Speed limits range from 25 mph in residential areas to 85 mph on one Texas toll road — the widest spread of any country in this registry. Specific to the United States

  • The United States is party to the 1949 Geneva Convention.

  • AAA and AATA are the only two organisations authorised by the U.S. Department of State to issue US International Driving Permits.

United States driving document questions

Do you need an International Driving Permit to drive in the United States?

It depends on the state and on your rental provider. Most states allow a visitor to drive on a valid foreign licence for a limited period. A licence not written in English is where a translation or IDP becomes practically necessary, and rental company policy is often stricter than state law.

Can I drive in the US with a UK, EU or Australian licence?

Generally yes for a limited period, and because those licences are in English no translation is normally needed. Check the specific state you will be driving in, and confirm your rental provider's own documentation policy separately.

Who issues International Driving Permits in the United States?

The American Automobile Association and the American Automobile Touring Alliance. They are the only two organisations authorised by the U.S. Department of State. Both issue to holders of a valid US state licence.

Can a foreign visitor get an IDP from AAA while in the United States?

No. An International Driving Permit must be issued in the country that issued your driving licence. A UK licence holder needs a UK-issued IDP, obtained before travelling.

Is a digital IDP accepted in the United States?

No US state has published a policy accepting a digital-only driving document, and AAA states that digital IDPs are not available. Treat printed as the only reliable format.

What is the drink-drive limit in the United States?

0.08 per cent in 49 states. Utah applies 0.05 per cent, the strictest in the country. Every state applies zero or near-zero tolerance to drivers under 21.

What are the speed limits in the United States?

They vary by state and road type, posted in miles per hour: roughly 25 mph residential, 55 to 70 mph on rural highways and interstates, with a maximum of 85 mph on one Texas toll road. Posted signs govern.

How long can I drive in the US on a foreign licence?

The permitted period is set by each state rather than federally, so there is no single answer. Check the rules of the specific state where you will be driving, and note that becoming a resident brings separate licensing requirements.

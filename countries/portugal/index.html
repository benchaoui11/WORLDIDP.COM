Do You Need an International Driving Permit in Portugal?

Licences issued outside the EU or EEA should be accompanied by an International Driving Permit or an official Portuguese translation. Portugal is party to the 1949 Geneva Convention and 1968 Vienna Convention. Traffic drives on the right. Motorway limit 120 km/h. Sources reviewed 2026-07-23.

Who needs an IDP in Portugal

Who this applies to. Licences issued outside the EU or EEA should be accompanied by an International Driving Permit or an official Portuguese translation

Who is treated differently. EU and EEA licence holders drive under EU mutual recognition rules

Portugal follows the EU pattern: EU and EEA licences are recognised, and licences from elsewhere are where an IDP or an official Portuguese translation becomes relevant. Post-Brexit UK licence holders should check current guidance rather than assume the pre-2021 position still applies.

Which convention applies

Portugal is party to the 1949 Geneva Convention and 1968 Vienna Convention. The convention determines which IDP format has a legal basis here, which is why two IDPs that look similar are not always interchangeable.

Where a country is party to more than one convention, the more recent convention generally governs between states that are party to both.

Is a digital IDP accepted in Portugal?

Not confirmed. No published Portuguese national policy confirming acceptance of digital-only driving documents was found. Carry a printed copy.

This is the least documented question in this category. Most national authorities have not published a position on digital-only driving documents, and rental companies set their own rules. Carry a printed document and treat any digital copy as a convenience, not a substitute. See our explanation of what an IDP is for more.

What to carry

  • Original valid national driving licence
  • International Driving Permit for non-EU/EEA licences
  • Passport or national ID
  • Vehicle documents and insurance

Your original national driving licence is the document that grants driving privileges. A translation-support document is presented alongside it and never replaces it.

Rental desks and roadside checks in Portugal

At the rental desk. Portuguese rental firms commonly ask non-EU visitors for a translation at collection, particularly in the Algarve and on Madeira. Electronic tolls are the bigger practical issue — most motorways use the Via Verde system and rental companies charge an administration fee per toll passage.

In a roadside check. Speed enforcement is largely automated. The alcohol limit is tiered: 0.05 g/l generally, but 0.02 g/l for anyone licensed less than three years and for professional drivers — a stricter window than most of Europe applies.

Specific to Portugal

  • Portugal is party to both the 1949 Geneva and 1968 Vienna Conventions.
  • The 0.02 g/l limit applies for a full three years after licensing, longer than the equivalent rule in most EU states.
  • Most motorways use electronic tolling; rental vehicles need a transponder or the company bills tolls afterwards with a fee.
  • Madeira and the Azores have their own road conditions and steep gradients that affect vehicle choice.

Portugal driving document questions

Do you need an International Driving Permit in Portugal?

EU and EEA licence holders drive under mutual recognition rules. If your licence was issued outside that framework, Portuguese guidance points to an IDP or an official Portuguese translation carried with the original.

How do motorway tolls work for rental cars in Portugal?

Most Portuguese motorways use electronic tolling rather than cash booths. Rental vehicles either carry a transponder or the company bills the tolls to you afterwards with an administration fee. Confirm which applies at collection.

What is the drink-drive limit in Portugal?

0.05 g/l for most drivers. Drivers licensed for less than three years and professional drivers face a stricter 0.02 g/l limit.

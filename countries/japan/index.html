Do You Need an International Driving Permit in Japan?

Japan recognises only IDPs issued under the 1949 Geneva Convention, with separate translation rules for a limited set of foreign licences. Japan is party to the 1949 Geneva Convention. Traffic drives on the left. Sources reviewed 2026-07-23.

Who needs an IDP in Japan

Who this applies to. Japan recognises only IDPs issued under the 1949 Geneva Convention, with separate translation rules for a limited set of foreign licences.

Who is treated differently. Some licence holders from designated jurisdictions may drive with an authorised Japanese translation instead of an IDP.

Japan is the clearest example of why the convention matters. Japan recognises IDPs issued under the 1949 Geneva Convention only. An IDP issued under the 1968 Vienna Convention is not the accepted route here. Holders of licences from a small set of designated jurisdictions — including Switzerland, Germany, France, Belgium, Monaco and Taiwan — use an authorised Japanese translation instead.

Which convention applies

Japan is party to the 1949 Geneva Convention. The convention determines which IDP format has a legal basis here, which is why two IDPs that look similar are not always interchangeable.

Is a digital IDP accepted in Japan?

Not confirmed. No confirmed official national policy was found. Carry the printed document and original licence when a physical document is requested.

This is the least documented question in this category. Most national authorities have not published a position on digital-only driving documents, and rental companies set their own rules. Carry a printed document and treat any digital copy as a convenience, not a substitute. See our explanation of what an IDP is for more.

What to carry

  • Original valid driving licence
  • Passport or travel ID where requested
  • IDP, official translation, or private translation-support document where applicable

Your original national driving licence is the document that grants driving privileges. A translation-support document is presented alongside it and never replaces it.

Rental desks and roadside checks in Japan

At the rental desk. Japanese rental companies check the IDP format at collection and will decline a vehicle if the document is not the 1949 Geneva form or an accepted translation. This is the single most common reason visitors are refused a rental car in Japan.

In a roadside check. Traffic policing is administered by prefectural police under the National Police Agency framework. You must carry the original licence together with the IDP or authorised translation.

Specific to Japan

  • Japan recognises the 1949 Geneva Convention IDP only — the 1968 Vienna form is not the accepted route.
  • Designated jurisdictions use an authorised Japanese translation instead of an IDP.
  • Japan drives on the left, which catches out visitors from most of continental Europe and the Americas.

Japan driving document questions

Do I need an International Driving Permit in Japan?

Yes for most visitors, and the format matters: Japan recognises IDPs issued under the 1949 Geneva Convention. A 1968 Vienna Convention IDP is not the accepted route.

Can I drive in Japan with a 1968 Vienna Convention IDP?

No. Japanese police guidance identifies the 1949 Geneva Convention as the recognised basis. Holders of licences from certain designated jurisdictions use an authorised Japanese translation instead.

Which side of the road does Japan drive on?

Japan drives on the left.

Is a digital IDP accepted in Japan?

We found no published Japanese policy accepting digital-only documents, and rental companies inspect the physical document at collection. Carry the printed original.

Do You Need an International Driving Permit in the United Kingdom?

Who actually needs an IDP in the United Kingdom

Who this applies to. Visitors holding a full, valid driving licence from any country can generally drive small vehicles listed on that licence in Great Britain for up to 12 months from the date they last entered, without an International Driving Permit

Who is treated differently. Northern Ireland applies its own rules, and longer stays or a move to GB residency trigger separate licence-exchange requirements rather than the visitor rule

The United Kingdom is the case that reverses the usual question. If you are visiting, your own full national licence is normally what you drive on — GOV.UK sets a 12-month window for visitors driving small vehicles listed on that licence, and an International Driving Permit is not the mechanism that grants it. The IDP question in a UK context is far more often the other direction: UK licence holders driving abroad, where some destinations now expect one.

Which convention applies

The United Kingdom is party to the 1926 Paris Convention, 1949 Geneva Convention and 1968 Vienna Convention. The convention determines which IDP format has a legal basis here, which is why two IDPs that look similar are not always interchangeable.

Where a country is party to more than one convention, the more recent convention generally governs between states that are party to both.

Is a digital IDP accepted in the United Kingdom?

Not confirmed. No published UK national policy confirming acceptance of digital-only driving documents was found. Because visitors can generally drive on their own national licence, the practical requirement is the original licence itself rather than any translation.

This is the least documented question in this category. Most national authorities have not published a position on digital-only driving documents, and rental companies set their own rules. Carry a printed document and treat any digital copy as a convenience, not a substitute. See our explanation of what an IDP is for more.

What to carry

  • Your original, full, valid national driving licence
  • Passport or photographic ID where a hire company requests it
  • Proof of insurance cover for the vehicle you are driving
  • V5C logbook or hire agreement for the vehicle

Your original national driving licence is the document that grants driving privileges. A translation-support document is presented alongside it and never replaces it.

Rental desks and roadside checks in the United Kingdom

At the rental desk. Hire companies are the constraint here, not the law. UK rental firms set their own minimum age (commonly 21 to 25), apply young-driver surcharges, and can require a licence check code from DVLA for GB licence holders. A hire desk asking for a translation of a non-Latin-alphabet licence is applying company policy, not a legal requirement.

In a roadside check. Speed enforcement is heavily camera-based and limits are posted in miles per hour, which catches out drivers arriving from mainland Europe. Note the split drink-drive limit: England, Wales and Northern Ireland use 80 mg per 100 ml of blood, while Scotland uses 50 mg. Driving from Carlisle into Scotland changes the limit that applies to you.

Specific to the United Kingdom

  • Visitors can generally drive on a full valid overseas licence for up to 12 months from the date they last entered Great Britain.
  • Northern Ireland operates its own licensing rules separately from Great Britain.
  • Speed limits are posted in miles per hour, not kilometres per hour.
  • Scotland's drink-drive limit is lower than the rest of the UK: 50 mg per 100 ml against 80 mg.
  • Traffic drives on the left, and most hire cars are manual unless an automatic is booked specifically.
  • The UK is party to the 1926 Paris, 1949 Geneva and 1968 Vienna Conventions.

United Kingdom driving document questions

Do you need an International Driving Permit to drive in the UK?

Generally no. GOV.UK guidance allows a visitor holding a full, valid licence from any country to drive small vehicles listed on that licence in Great Britain for up to 12 months from the date they last entered. The permit is not what grants that permission.

How long can I drive in the UK on a foreign licence?

Up to 12 months from the date you last entered Great Britain, for the small vehicle categories shown on your licence. Longer stays and a move to GB residency bring separate licence-exchange rules into play.

Is a licence translation useful in the UK at all?

It can be, but as a practical aid rather than a legal requirement. If your licence is not in the Latin alphabet, a hire desk or an officer may find a translation easier to read. That is a company or convenience matter, not a condition of driving.

Which side of the road does the UK drive on?

The left. Speed limits are posted in miles per hour: 30 mph in built-up areas, 60 mph on single carriageways and 70 mph on dual carriageways and motorways, subject to posted signs.

Is the drink-drive limit the same across the UK?

No. England, Wales and Northern Ireland apply 80 mg of alcohol per 100 ml of blood. Scotland applies a lower limit of 50 mg. Crossing the border changes the limit that applies to you.

Do UK licence holders need an IDP to drive abroad?

For some destinations, yes. This is the more common UK-related IDP question, and the answer depends entirely on the destination and which road traffic convention it recognises.

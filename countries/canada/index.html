Driving in Canada: What to Confirm Before You Travel

We have not completed source-backed research for Canada, so this page does not state whether an International Driving Permit is required. Canada drives on the right. Canada is party to the 1949 Geneva Convention. Confirm current requirements with the relevant national authority and your rental provider before you drive.

Which convention applies

Canada is party to the 1949 Geneva Convention. The convention determines which IDP format has a legal basis here, which is why two IDPs that look similar are not always interchangeable.

Is a digital IDP accepted in Canada?

Not confirmed. No published Canadian policy accepting digital-only driving documents was found, and the rules are provincial rather than national. Carry the original licence.

This is the least documented question in this category. Most national authorities have not published a position on digital-only driving documents, and rental companies set their own rules. Carry a printed document and treat any digital copy as a convenience, not a substitute. See our explanation of what an IDP is for more.

What to carry

  • Original valid national driving licence
  • IDP or translation where the licence is not in English or French
  • Passport
  • Proof of insurance — mandatory minimums differ by province

Your original national driving licence is the document that grants driving privileges. A translation-support document is presented alongside it and never replaces it.

Rental desks and roadside checks in Canada

At the rental desk. Insurance minimums are provincial, and some provinces run public auto insurance systems that work differently from private cover elsewhere. Cross-border trips into the United States are usually permitted but must be declared. Winter tyre rules differ by province — Quebec mandates them seasonally.

In a roadside check. The federal criminal threshold is 0.08 per cent, but provinces layer administrative penalties on top from 0.05 per cent — licence suspension at 0.05 is common even though it is not a criminal offence. Most provinces apply zero tolerance to novice and young drivers regardless.

Specific to Canada

  • Canada is party to the 1949 Geneva Convention.
  • Rules for visiting drivers are set provincially, not federally — the permitted period differs by province.
  • The federal criminal alcohol threshold is 0.08 per cent, but provincial administrative penalties commonly begin at 0.05 per cent.
  • Quebec mandates winter tyres seasonally; other provinces do not.
  • A licence in English or French generally needs no translation.

Why this page does not state a requirement

Confirm with the national road authority or embassy for Canada, and separately with your rental provider, whose contractual rules can be stricter than the law.

Canada driving document questions

Do you need an International Driving Permit in Canada?

It depends on the province and on how long you are staying. Visitors may generally drive on a valid foreign licence for a limited period, commonly around 90 days. A licence already in English or French generally needs no translation.

Is the drink-drive limit in Canada 0.08?

0.08 per cent is the federal criminal threshold, but that is not the whole picture. Provinces impose administrative penalties including licence suspension from 0.05 per cent, and most apply zero tolerance to novice and young drivers.

Do I need winter tyres in Canada?

Quebec mandates them seasonally. Other provinces do not require them by law, though conditions frequently make them necessary in practice.

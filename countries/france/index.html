Do You Need an International Driving Permit in France?

Drivers with licences that are not in French or are not covered by recognition arrangements should carry a translation or IDP. France is party to the 1949 Geneva Convention and 1968 Vienna Convention. Traffic drives on the right. Motorway limit 130 km/h. Sources reviewed 2026-07-23.

Who needs an IDP in France

Who this applies to. Drivers with licences that are not in French or are not covered by recognition arrangements should carry a translation or IDP.

Who is treated differently. EU/EEA licence holders are generally treated separately from other visitors.

France distinguishes between licences issued inside the EU/EEA and everything else. The practical trigger is language and format: if your licence is not in French and is not covered by a recognition arrangement, a translation or IDP is what French guidance points to.

Which convention applies

France is party to the 1949 Geneva Convention and 1968 Vienna Convention. The convention determines which IDP format has a legal basis here, which is why two IDPs that look similar are not always interchangeable.

Where a country is party to more than one convention, the more recent convention generally governs between states that are party to both.

Is a digital IDP accepted in France?

Not confirmed. No confirmed official national policy was found. Carry the printed document and original licence when a physical document is requested.

This is the least documented question in this category. Most national authorities have not published a position on digital-only driving documents, and rental companies set their own rules. Carry a printed document and treat any digital copy as a convenience, not a substitute. See our explanation of what an IDP is for more.

What to carry

  • Original valid driving licence
  • Passport or travel ID where requested
  • IDP, official translation, or private translation-support document where applicable

Your original national driving licence is the document that grants driving privileges. A translation-support document is presented alongside it and never replaces it.

Rental desks and roadside checks in France

At the rental desk. French rental companies routinely ask non-EU visitors for a translation document at collection, especially at airport and rail-station branches. This is a contractual requirement set by the company.

In a roadside check. France operates extensive automated speed enforcement. Officers expect the original licence; a translation is supporting material. Speed limits on rural roads were reduced to 80 km/h and some departments have since restored 90 km/h on selected routes, so posted signs govern.

Specific to France

  • France is party to both the 1949 Geneva and 1968 Vienna Conventions.
  • Rural single-carriageway limits vary by department between 80 and 90 km/h — the posted sign is authoritative.
  • Motorway limits drop in rain and low visibility.

France driving document questions

Do I need an International Driving Permit in France?

If your licence is issued in the EU or EEA, it is handled under EU recognition rules. If your licence is issued elsewhere or is not written in French, French guidance points to carrying a translation or IDP alongside the original.

Can I drive in France with a US or Canadian licence?

These licences sit outside the EU/EEA framework, so a translation document is the route described. Carry it with the original licence.

Is a digital IDP accepted in France?

We found no published French national policy confirming acceptance of digital-only documents. Carry a printed copy and confirm with your rental provider.

What is the motorway speed limit in France?

130 km/h in dry conditions, reduced in rain, with 50 km/h in built-up areas. Rural limits vary between 80 and 90 km/h by department.

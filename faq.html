The essentials

Start here — what an IDP is and whether your trip needs one.

An IDP is a translation-support document for your national driver's license, prepared in multiple languages. It does not replace your home license — it travels beside it, so police and foreign authorities abroad can read your credentials more easily. With WorldIDP, that document is prepared and delivered digitally, ready to use with your valid national licence.

They're the same document under different names. There is no standalone “international license” that lets you drive worldwide on its own — every legitimate version is an IDP that accompanies your valid national license. If a seller promises a permit that fully replaces your home license, treat that as a red flag, not a feature.

You may need an IDP or licence translation when driving outside the country that issued your licence, especially if your licence is not in the local language or Latin alphabet. Requirements vary by destination, so check the country rules before you travel.

It depends on where you're going. Some countries require it by law for any foreign driver; others accept your national license alone but still allow local authorities to request an IDP. Because the rule shifts at the border — and sometimes before travel checks — the safe move is to carry one.
